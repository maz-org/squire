Thesis

An attendant at your elbow, not an oracle from the high seat.

Squire is a knight's squire — carrying the gear, tending the horse, remembering the rulebook, knowing the inventory, handing over the right item at the right moment. The design language is a squire's personal ledger: warm ink, cream parchment, a single wax-seal red, literary serifs, and one small illuminated capital on every answer. Modern restraint in the vocabulary of a treasured reference book.

You asked

What does the Poison condition do?

Poison is a negative condition. While poisoned, all attacks against you deal +1 damage until cleansed. Removed by Heal X actions or by the Restoration ability. Apply Poison via the icon shown on monster attack abilities — see Rulebook p.47 for the full list of conditions and Scenario 14 for an example of poison-heavy encounters.

What items should I bring to tonight's scenario?

Given your Rage build at level 4 and the party's current composition, three items earn their slots tonight. Bring Heater Shield for the elite Flame Demons you'll encounter in Scenario 14 — the shield 1 reduces their Fire damage by enough to survive a full round. Pair it with Boots of Striding for the mobility your melee build needs to reach the altar before it breaks. Leave Minor Healing Potion at the outpost; your new Restoration ability covers that slot. See Rulebook p.47 and Scenario Book §14.

Color — Rubrication

Warm ink, cream parchment, one wax-seal red.

Medieval manuscripts used red ink (rubrication) for initial capitals, rubrics, and important marks — sparingly, against warm vellum. That's the whole color story. Five roles, one accent, zero gradients.

Typography

Two fonts, two jobs.

A literary serif carries Squire's signature moments — the wordmark, the hero question, the drop cap on every answer. A crisp humanist sans handles everything else, optimized for scanning at the table. Both on Google Fonts, neither in the blacklist, zero Inter.
